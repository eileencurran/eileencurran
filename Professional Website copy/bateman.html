PRSSA Bateman Case Study Competition 2017

During the 2017 spring semester, I had the opportunity to participate in that National Public Relations Student Society of America Bateman Case Study Competition. To do so, I enrolled in the Bateman section of STC 359 in the fall. At the end of the semester, of professor, Shara Clark, evaluated each team's plans as well as the enthusiasm and participation of each student throughout the course. I was fortunate enough to be selected, along with four of my peers, to participate in the national competiton in the spring.

PRSSA partnered with the Campaign to Change Direction for the 2017 competiton. The Campaign to Change Direction is a nonprofit organization that aims to change the culture of mental health in America and encourages people to pay attention to their emotional well-being and prioritize it as much as physical well-being.

The five signs of mental suffering are: personality change, agitation, withdrawal, poor self-care and hopelessness. With these in mind, my team and I created and implemented a complete public relations campaign on Miami University's campus and named it Take 5 Miami. The campaign included social media posts, PR efforts and events on behalf of the Campaign to Change Direction, and a schoolwide pledge which yielded over 1,000 signatures from faculty/staff and students.
